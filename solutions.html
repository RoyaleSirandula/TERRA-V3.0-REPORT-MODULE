Section 01

Target
Customers

Large-Scale Conservation NGOs
Organisations like Save the Elephants managing vast protected territories that span hundreds of thousands of hectares, operating with limited ranger coverage and disconnected field data systems.
Core Need
Real-time, AI-verified intelligence to eliminate fragmented data silos and enable non-technical teams to make instant, evidence-backed field decisions.
Government Wildlife Authorities
National parks agencies and wildlife services responsible for enforcing conservation law across protected areas — often burdened by analog VHF radio systems and spreadsheet-era reporting workflows.
Core Need
Drastically reduced response times to poaching and HWC incidents, replacing hours of latency with seconds-level situational awareness.
Local & Private Conservancies
Community conservancies and private reserves managing human-wildlife boundaries — where livestock losses, crop destruction, and human casualties from preventable conflict erode community support for conservation.
Why Terra Matters Here
Terra converts communities from passive bystanders into active intelligence contributors. Farmers and village leaders submit real-time sightings, receive early-warning alerts, and gain a direct stake in conservation outcomes — dramatically reducing conflict and building long-term buy-in.

Section 03

Prototype
Evolution

Early Prototype
Data Dashboard & API Aggregation
Terra's early prototype was a simple dashboard aggregating wildlife sightings and migration data from open APIs. The focus was proving that data could be collected, structured, and displayed in a unified geographic interface.
Completed
Version 1
Data Visualisation & Pattern Mapping
Version 1 tested data visualisation and pattern mapping but lacked real-time relevance and field usability. The key lesson: visual insight without immediate tactical action has limited value on the ground.
"It looks good, but how does this help me respond on the ground?" — Field Ranger
Completed
Version 2 — Current
Structured Reporting, Alert Layers & Confidence Scoring
Version 2 introduced structured reporting and alert layers, integrating direct ranger inputs alongside basic anomaly detection. This drastically improved situational awareness and made outputs immediately actionable for field decisions. We are now refining real-time alerts, confidence scoring, and site analysis tools.
In Active Development
Next Phase
Live Operations Validation
We still need to validate long-term platform adoption with rangers and local authorities during live operations in bandwidth-constrained conservation environments — the true proving ground for sustainable field deployment.
Planned — Field Trials
